BEANS

The profile of the Nigerian Beans

There are over a 100 varieties of beans known to man. Some of these are specific to certain regions of the world. The popular Nigerian brown beans are actually brown cowpeas which are cultivated and consumed in most of Sub-saharan Africa. This variety also exists as white cowpea. The beans are highly nutritious containing good levels of carbohydrate, protein, dietary fibre and minerals such as calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, iron, zinc, manganese and copper. Brown beans are an important crop which plays a significant role in the diets of Nigerians. They serve as a major source of protein in the absence of sufficient animal protein. There are two further categories of the brown beans namely Oloyin and Olo 1&2. The Oloyin beans have a naturally sweet taste and preferred in certain dishes. Ingredients Measurements
Honey beans/Nigerian beans 4 cups
Palm oil 0.25 cups
Roma tomatoes 4
Scotch bonnet peppers 4
Large red onions 1
Large ball pepper 1
Ground dried shrimp(cray fish) 5 tbsp
Shrimp bullion 5 tsp
Salt to taste 1.5 tsp

    Steps to prepare beans

  1. Rinse the beans and place in a deep pot with seven cups of water
  2. Boil the beans on medium heat until it becomes soft, it could take about 40 minutes.
  3. While the beans are boiling, blend the tomatoes, peppers, and onions and set aside
  4. Once the beans are softened from boiling (do not drain the water), turn the heat to low, then pour the blended tomatoes into the pot
  5. Allow the beans to cook for an additional 10 minutes with the tomatoes, then add in the crayfish, bullion and palm oil. Stir and cook for another 10 minutes
  6. Season the beans with salt to your taste. Turn off the heat, and allow to sit for 5 minutes before serving.
